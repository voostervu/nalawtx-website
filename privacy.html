Privacy Policy

This privacy policy describes how Nguyen & Associates Injury Law Firm ("we," "our," "us") collects, uses, and protects information about people who visit nalawtx.com or contact us about a potential legal matter.

Information we collect

Information you give us. When you fill out a form on this website, call us, email us, or otherwise contact our firm, you may provide:

  • Your name
  • Your phone number
  • Your email address
  • A description of your legal situation
  • Your preferred language

Information we collect automatically. Like most websites, we collect basic technical information when you visit nalawtx.com:

  • Your IP address (used to determine general geographic location and prevent fraud)
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system
  • Pages you view and how you arrived at our site
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see "Cookies" below)

How we use your information

We use information you provide to:

  • Respond to your inquiry about a potential legal matter
  • Schedule a free consultation
  • Determine whether we can represent you (conflicts check)
  • Communicate with you about your case if we agree to represent you
  • Comply with our legal and ethical obligations

We use automatically collected information to:

  • Operate, maintain, and improve nalawtx.com
  • Understand how visitors find and use our site
  • Detect and prevent fraud or abuse
  • Comply with legal requirements

Confidentiality of legal inquiries

Important: Submitting a form or sending us a message does not create an attorney-client relationship. An attorney-client relationship is created only when both you and our firm sign a written engagement agreement.

That said, we treat all inquiries as confidential. Information you submit is reviewed only by attorneys and authorized staff at Nguyen & Associates for the purpose of evaluating whether we can represent you. We do not share inquiry information with third parties except as described below or as required by law.

How we share information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We share information only in these circumstances:

  • Service providers: Vendors who help us operate the website and our practice (form processing, email, phone tracking, hosting). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your information.
  • Legal requirements: When required by law, court order, or to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.
  • If we represent you: Information necessary to handle your case may be shared with parties involved in your matter (insurance companies, opposing counsel, courts, expert witnesses, medical providers) as part of normal legal representation.

Cookies and tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies for:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the site to function (always on)
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use the site (you can opt out)
  • Phone tracking: Some phone numbers on our site are tracking numbers that route to our main line. This tells us which marketing channel referred your call but does not record the call.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but parts of the site may not work properly.

Your rights

Depending on your state, you may have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we have collected about you
  • Request a copy of your personal information
  • Request that we delete your personal information
  • Opt out of certain types of data sharing

To exercise these rights, email us at info@nalawtx.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Data retention

We retain inquiry information for as long as necessary to:

  • Evaluate potential representation (typically 30 days for inquiries we don't take on)
  • Provide legal services to clients we represent
  • Comply with our professional record-keeping obligations under Texas Disciplinary Rules
  • Defend against potential claims

Children's privacy

Our website is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided information to us, please contact us at info@nalawtx.com.

Security

We use reasonable security measures to protect information transmitted to us. However, no website transmission can be guaranteed 100% secure. Submitting information through our website is at your own risk.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of nalawtx.com after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
